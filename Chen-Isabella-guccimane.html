Childhood/ Start

Rapper, Radric Delantic Davis aka Gucci Mane was born on February 12, 1980 in Bessemer, Alabama. His older half-brother Victor introduced Gucci to hip-hop for the first time when Gucci was only six years old by bringing Gucci to a Run DMC, Beastie Boys and LL Cool J concert. Gucci enjoyed writing poetry as a child, and began rapping at the age of 14. Following his first arrest, Gucci began to take music seriously and released La Flare on Str8 Drop Records. It was pressed onto about 1000 CDs and distributed throughout East Atlanta.

Career

Following the release of La Flare, and being inspired by Master P, Gucci decided he wanted to start a music label. Gucci began to manage rapper, Lil Buddy in 2001 and eventually met producer Zaytoven (who would go on to collaborate with Gucci in the future). In 2002, Gucci linked up with SYS Records as a member of the Sign Yourself Click. Gucci also linked up with Zaytoven and made his own label, LaFlare Entertainment.

Success

In 2005, Gucci Mane debuted with Trap House, followed by his second and third albums, Hard to Kill and Trap-A-Thon in 2006. His fourth album, Back to the Trap House was released in 2007. Then, in 2007, Gucci Mane founded 1017 Records. Pioneering the hip hop subgenre trap music, alongside fellow Atlanta-based rappers T.I. and Young Jeezy. In 2009, Gucci Mane released his sixth studio album, The State vs. Radric Gucci, going on to become his first gold certified album. His 2016 collaboration with Rae Sremmurd, titled "Black Beatles", provided Gucci Mane with his first number-one single on the US Billboard Hot 100 chart.

2017

In August 2017, Gucci announced his eleventh studio album, "Mr. Davis”. He's been keeping a low profile; working hard and staying healthy. Gucci married Keyshia Ka’oir on October 17, 2017.
